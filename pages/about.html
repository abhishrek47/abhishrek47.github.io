The Story

I started in Learning & Development the way most people do — as an instructional designer creating content in the tools that existed. But I kept hitting the same wall: the authoring tools stopped where the real problems didn't.

So I learned to code. I learned to think like a technologist. I built systems that don't just deliver learning, but make sense of it — systems that scale, systems that integrate with the infrastructure that organizations actually use.

Now I work at the intersection. I understand ADDIE and Bloom as deeply as I understand JavaScript and APIs. I can translate between the language of instructional design and the language of technology. That translation is rare, and it's valuable.
