What I actually did

My entire job was to describe what I wanted and tell Claude when something looked wrong. That's it.

1

I told Claude what I wanted to build

Described the video — 25 seconds, my brand colours, 5 scenes, what each scene should say and do. That was my entire input. Claude figured out how to build it.

Me
Build me a 25-second marketing video for my newsletter. Navy background, red accent. Scene 1: title card. Scene 2: the problem statement with a strikethrough animation. Scene 3: a Venn diagram showing the gap between L&D and engineering. Scene 4: a comparison table. Scene 5: CTA. Export as MP4.
Claude
On it. I'll build the video by generating each frame as an image, then stitching them together into a video file. Setting up the scenes now with smooth transitions between each one — rendering...
2

Claude rendered the video — I reviewed it

Claude ran the code in the background and produced the MP4. I watched it and noted what looked off.

Me
Some issues. The strikethrough line goes way past the text. The Venn diagram labels are clipping outside the circles. The icon and text in the comparison table aren't aligned.
Claude
Found all three. Strikethrough line — fixed, it was extending beyond the text. Venn labels — fixed, moved them to where they fit inside the circles. Icon and text alignment — fixed, they're now centred against each other. Re-rendering now...
3

We iterated until it looked right

A few more rounds — column headers too small, crossfade looked murky, animation timing felt slow. Each time: I described what was wrong, Claude diagnosed and fixed it.

Me
Column headings font is too small. Also the crossfade between scenes looks wrong — it gets darker instead of dissolving cleanly.
Claude
Headings made larger. The dark crossfade was a layering issue — both scenes were fading simultaneously which made the transition go dark instead of dissolving cleanly. Fixed the layering order. Rendering now...
4

Final video — done

25 seconds. Full HD. 0.7 MB. Ready to upload. I didn't touch the code once — just described what I wanted and what needed fixing.
